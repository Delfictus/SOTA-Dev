Platform Architecture

End-to-end GPU-native pipeline from raw PDB to pharmacophore-annotated binding sites

⚡

Neuromorphic Spike Detection

Multi-modal UV pump-probe (258/274nm), laser-induced fluorescence (LIF), and electrostatic field perturbation (EFP) channels detect transient pocket-opening events across molecular dynamics trajectories in real time.

⚙

GPU-Accelerated MD Engine

Custom AMBER ff14SB integrator with 87 hand-tuned CUDA kernels. Multi-stream execution runs 10 independent trajectories simultaneously on a single GPU. OptiX 9.1 ray-traced solvent boundary.

Cryptic Pocket Detection Accuracy

Evaluated on 32 CryptoBench targets (ongoing — full 222-target evaluation in progress). The closest predicted pocket centroid falls within 4 Angstroms of the experimentally determined cryptic site in 53.1% of cases at Top-1 rank.

At Top-3 rank, accuracy rises to 75.0%, demonstrating strong recall of the true cryptic pocket within the top-ranked predictions.

Dataset: CryptoBench (Meller et al. 2023) · Metric: Distance to Closest Atom (DCA)

What Makes This Different

Traditional pocket detectors (fpocket, SiteMap, P2Rank) operate on static crystal structures and cannot detect cryptic pockets by definition — the pocket doesn't exist in the apo conformation.

PRISM-4D runs GPU-accelerated molecular dynamics to sample conformational fluctuations, then applies neuromorphic spike detection to identify transient pocket-opening events that would be invisible to static methods.

Target Applications

🏭

Undruggable Oncoproteins

Discover cryptic pockets on high-value targets like MYC, RAS, and p53 that lack traditional binding sites in crystal structures. Enable small-molecule intervention where none existed before.

🧬

Protein-Protein Interfaces

Detect transient pockets at PPI interfaces during conformational breathing. Identify druggable hotspots on flat, featureless surfaces that appear only during dynamic fluctuations.

Map allosteric cryptic sites distal to the active site. Enable mechanism-of-action diversity through non-competitive binding site identification.
